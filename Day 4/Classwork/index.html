Main Information

Dogs (Canis lupus familiaris) are domesticated mammals, often called "man's best friend." They’ve been living alongside humans for thousands of years and have been bred into hundreds of different breeds with various shapes, sizes, and personalities.


Description

Dogs are descendants of wolves, and their domestication is believed to have started at least 15,000 to 30,000 years ago. Early humans and wolves probably formed a mutually beneficial relationship: wolves got food scraps, and humans got protection and hunting help. Through selective breeding over centuries, humans shaped wolves into today’s domestic dogs — everything from tiny Chihuahuas to giant Great Danes.

Temperament

Dogs are incredibly diverse, but generally: They’re loyal, affectionate, and eager to please. Many breeds are social and love being around people or other dogs. Temperament can vary a lot by breed: Retrievers are friendly and playful. Border Collies are super smart and energetic. Bulldogs are chill and cuddly. Training and environment also play a big role in shaping a dog’s behavior.




























Health

Dogs need a mix of good food, exercise, mental stimulation, and vet care. Here are some basics: Lifespan: Depends on breed, but most live 10–15 years. Common health issues: Joint problems like hip dysplasia Dental issues Skin allergies Obesity from too much food/little activity Care tips: Regular walks and playtime Balanced diet Vet checkups Vaccinations and parasite prevention
